Salangai Pooja & Arangetram

The Salangai Pooja is the sacred ceremony in which the student's ankle bells are formally tied for the first time — a public performance marking their initiation as a classical dancer. Learn more →

The Arangetram — "ascending the stage" — is the full-length classical solo debut, typically 2.5–3 hours. The pinnacle milestone in a student's journey. Learn more →

Typically 4–6 years of consistent training. The Salangai Pooja must be completed first. Readiness is assessed by Guru Deepa alone.

Yes — completely. Dance training, music, orchestra, costume, ceremonial guidance, and rehearsals. We have guided 5+ Arangetrams.

Online Classes

All live online classes via Google Meet. A free Google account is all you need.

Online learning at Kalpavruksha is designed to be thorough and personal. Students submit weekly practice videos, which Guru Deepa reviews and provides detailed corrections on. This structured video feedback, combined with live sessions, ensures consistent progress. Students across the UK, USA, and India have progressed excellently through this format.
