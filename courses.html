Beginners Bharatanatyam

The perfect introduction to classical Bharatanatyam. For children from age 4, this course builds essential foundations: posture, hand gestures, footwork, and an intuitive sense of rhythm.

Children learn through stories from mythology and simple exercises. The environment is joyful and encouraging.

Suitable forChildren aged 4+
Sessions per week2 sessions per week
Duration1 hour per session
ModeIn-person / Online

Year 1 highlights

  • Aramandi and body alignment
  • Single and double adavus (footwork sequences)
  • Asamyukta hasta mudras
  • Basic nritta items: Alarippu, Jatiswaram
  • Introduction to tala: Adi, Rupaka
  • Participation in Salangai Pooja ceremony

2–5 years experience

Intermediate Bharatanatyam

Where Bharatanatyam truly opens up — from pure nritta (abstract dance) to nritya (expressive storytelling). Students begin working on longer, more complex compositions.

Abhinaya — facial expression and emotional portrayal — is introduced through padams and javalis from the Bhakti tradition.

PrerequisitesBeginner level completion
Sessions per week2–3 sessions per week
Duration1 hour per session
ModeIn-person / Online

Curriculum highlights

  • Complex adavu combinations and speed variations
  • Samyukta hasta mudras and Navarasas
  • Varnam — centrepiece of the repertoire
  • Padams and Javalis — abhinaya compositions
  • Thillana — the rhythmic finale
  • Stage performance participation

Sacred milestone

Salangai Pooja — The First Stage Debut

The Salangai Pooja is one of the most sacred rituals in a Bharatanatyam student's journey. It is the formal ceremony in which the salangai (ankle bells) are tied to the student's feet for the first time — a deeply symbolic act marking their initiation as a classical dancer.

In the guru-shishya tradition, the Salangai Pooja is far more than a performance event. It is a spiritual offering — to the Guru, to the art form, and to the divine. Kalpavruksha has proudly guided over 20+ Salangai Poojas since 2014.

PrerequisiteApprox. 1–2 years training
Guided by us20+ Salangai Poojas
Programme durationApprox. 1–1.5 hours

The solo debut

Arangetram Preparation

The Arangetram — literally "ascending the stage" — is the pinnacle milestone. Following the Salangai Pooja, it is a full-length solo performance of 2.5–3 hours, marking the student's transition to an independent artist.

Kalpavruksha provides complete support — training, music, orchestra, costume, ceremonial protocols and rehearsals. We have guided 5+ Arangetrams.

PrerequisitesAdvanced + Salangai Pooja done
Prep time1–2+ years intensive
Performance2.5–3 hours full margam
Training neededTypically 6–10+ years
Complete margam: Pushpanjali, Alarippu, Jatiswaram, Shabdam, Varnam, Padams, Thillana, Mangalam. Full orchestration guidance included.
