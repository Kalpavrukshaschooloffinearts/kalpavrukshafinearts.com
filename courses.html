Beginners Bharatanatyam

The perfect introduction to classical Bharatanatyam. Designed for children who have never danced before, this course builds the essential foundations: body posture, hand gestures (mudras), basic footwork patterns (adavus), and an intuitive sense of rhythm (tala).

The environment is joyful and encouraging. Children learn through stories from mythology, simple exercises that strengthen the body, and a gradual introduction to the philosophical beauty of this ancient art form. By the end of the first year, students typically perform at the annual Salangai Pooja ceremony.

Suitable for Children aged 5 and above
Batch size Max 8 students
Classes per week 2–3 days
Duration per class 60–75 minutes

Year 1 curriculum highlights

  • Aramandi (basic sitting posture) and body alignment
  • Single, double, and complex adavus (footwork sequences)
  • Asamyukta hasta mudras (single-hand gestures)
  • Basic nritta items: Alarippu, Jatiswaram
  • Introduction to tala (rhythmic cycles): Adi, Rupaka
  • Performance at Salangai Pooja ceremony

Intermediate Bharatanatyam

For students who have completed foundational training and are ready to deepen their artistic expression. The intermediate level is where Bharatanatyam truly opens up — from pure nritta (abstract dance) to nritya (expressive storytelling).

Students begin working on longer, more complex compositions. Abhinaya — the art of facial expression and emotional portrayal — is introduced through padams and javalis drawn from the Bhakti tradition. This is also where performance stamina is built.

Prerequisites Beginner level completion
Batch size Max 8 students
Classes per week 3 days
Duration per class 75–90 minutes

Curriculum highlights

  • Complex adavu combinations and speed variations
  • Samyukta hasta mudras and Navarasas
  • Varnam — the centrepiece of any Bharatanatyam repertoire
  • Padams and Javalis — abhinaya compositions
  • Thillana — the rhythmic finale piece
  • Keertana and Shloka interpretations
  • Stage performance participation

The solo debut

Arangetram Preparation

The Arangetram — literally "ascending the stage" — is the most significant milestone in a Bharatanatyam student's life. It is a full-length solo performance, typically 2.5–3 hours, marking the transition from student to artist.

Kalpavruksha provides complete Arangetram support — not just dance training, but guidance on music selection, costume design, orchestra arrangement, venue selection, and the ceremonial protocols. We have guided over 50 successful Arangetrams.

Prerequisites Advanced level + Guru's assessment
Preparation time 12–24 months
Classes per week 4–5 days (intensive)
Performance duration 2.5–3 hours

What Kalpavruksha provides

  • Complete margam training: Pushpanjali, Alarippu, Jatiswaram, Shabdam, Varnam, Padams, Thillana, Mangalam
  • Full abhinaya coaching for each composition
  • Guidance on orchestra, nattuvanar, vocalist selection
  • Costume and jewellery consultation
  • Ceremonial ritual guidance (Guru puja)
  • Multiple dress rehearsals

Adult Beginners

Have you always wanted to learn Bharatanatyam but never found the right time or place? You are exactly who this course is for. We welcome adult beginners of any age — many of our students started in their 30s, 40s, and even 50s.

The adult course is paced differently from children's classes. The explanations are conceptual, the learning is systematic, and the atmosphere is warm and non-judgmental. Adults bring emotional maturity and dedication that often results in extraordinary progress.

Benefits of Bharatanatyam for adults

🧘 Mind-body connection

Improves posture, balance, and body awareness

🎭 Emotional expression

A profound outlet for creativity and emotion

🏃 Physical fitness

Strengthens legs, core, and improves flexibility

📿 Cultural connection

A deep dive into Indian classical arts and philosophy
