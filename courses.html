Beginners Bharatanatyam

The perfect introduction to classical Bharatanatyam. Designed for children aged 4 and above who have never danced before, this course builds the essential foundations: body posture, hand gestures (mudras), basic footwork patterns (adavus), and an intuitive sense of rhythm (tala).

The environment is joyful and encouraging. Children learn through stories from mythology, simple exercises that strengthen the body, and a gradual introduction to the philosophical beauty of this ancient art form. By the end of the first year, students typically participate in the annual Salangai Pooja ceremony.

Suitable for Children aged 4 and above
Classes per week 2 days (general)
Duration per class 1 hour
Mode In-person / Online

Year 1 curriculum highlights

  • Aramandi (basic sitting posture) and body alignment
  • Single, double, and introductory adavus (footwork sequences)
  • Asamyukta hasta mudras (single-hand gestures)
  • Basic nritta items: Alarippu, Jatiswaram
  • Introduction to tala (rhythmic cycles): Adi, Rupaka
  • Participation in Salangai Pooja ceremony

2–5 years experience

Intermediate Bharatanatyam

For students who have completed foundational training and are ready to deepen their artistic expression. The intermediate level is where Bharatanatyam truly opens up — from pure nritta (abstract dance) to nritya (expressive storytelling).

Students begin working on longer, more complex compositions. Abhinaya — the art of facial expression and emotional portrayal — is introduced through padams and javalis drawn from the Bhakti tradition. This is also where performance stamina is built.

Prerequisites Beginner level completion
Classes per week 2–3 days
Duration per class 1 hour
Mode In-person / Online

Curriculum highlights

  • Complex adavu combinations and speed variations
  • Samyukta hasta mudras and Navarasas
  • Varnam — the centrepiece of any Bharatanatyam repertoire
  • Padams and Javalis — abhinaya compositions
  • Thillana — the rhythmic finale piece
  • Keertana and Shloka interpretations
  • Stage performance participation

Sacred milestone

Salangai Pooja — The First Stage Debut

The Salangai Pooja is one of the most sacred rituals in a Bharatanatyam student's journey. It is the formal ceremony in which the salangai (ankle bells) are tied to the student's feet for the first time — a deeply symbolic act that marks the student's initiation as a classical dancer.

In the guru-shishya tradition, the Salangai Pooja is far more than a performance event. It is a spiritual offering — to the Guru, to the art form, and to the divine. The student bows before the salangai, receives the guru's blessings, and publicly demonstrates the foundational repertoire they have learnt.

At Kalpavruksha, the Salangai Pooja is treated as a major milestone. It is an annual event celebrated with great care, where eligible students present a short but meaningful programme before family, friends, and the school community. Kalpavruksha has proudly guided over 20+ Salangai Poojas since its founding.

Prerequisite Approx. 1–2 years of foundational training
Guided by Kalpavruksha 20+ Salangai Poojas
Programme duration Approx. 1–1.5 hours
Scheduling Planned as per programme needs

Why the Salangai Pooja matters

  • The student publicly performs for the first time in classical attire with ankle bells
  • The Guru formally initiates the student into classical Bharatanatyam practice
  • Builds the student's confidence and stage presence for future performances
  • A stepping stone towards the Arangetram — the full solo debut
  • An emotional milestone for both student and family

The solo debut

Arangetram Preparation

The Arangetram — literally "ascending the stage" — is the pinnacle milestone in a Bharatanatyam student's life. Following the Salangai Pooja, the Arangetram is a full-length solo performance, typically 2.5–3 hours, marking the transition from student to independent artist.

Kalpavruksha provides complete Arangetram support — dance training, music selection, costume design, orchestra arrangement, venue selection, and ceremonial protocols. We have guided 5+ Arangetrams with dedication and care. Arangetram schedules and intensive batches are planned as per individual programme needs, not regular batch timings.

Prerequisites Advanced level + Salangai Pooja + Guru's assessment
Preparation time Typically 1–2+ years of intensive work
Classes Intensive — planned as per programme needs
Performance duration 2.5–3 hours

What Kalpavruksha provides

  • Complete margam training: Pushpanjali, Alarippu, Jatiswaram, Shabdam, Varnam, Padams, Thillana, Mangalam
  • Full abhinaya coaching for each composition
  • Guidance on orchestra, nattuvanar, vocalist selection
  • Costume and jewellery consultation
  • Ceremonial ritual guidance (Guru puja)
  • Multiple dress rehearsals
Note: Arangetram preparation batches are planned individually based on the student's progress and programme requirements. The Salangai Pooja is an important prerequisite before the Arangetram journey begins.

Adult Beginners

Have you always wanted to learn Bharatanatyam but never found the right time or place? You are exactly who this course is for. We welcome adult beginners of any age — many of our students started in their 30s, 40s, and even 50s.
