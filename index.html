About us

A living lineage of
classical Bharatanatyam

Kalpavruksha School of Fine Arts was founded in 2014 by Smt. Deepa Sadasivam, an alumna of the renowned Kalakshetra Foundation, Chennai. Like the mythical Kalpavriksha — the wish-fulfilling tree — this school exists to nourish every student's deepest artistic aspirations.

We follow the Kalakshetra bani — a style defined by its devotional purity, sculptural precision, and adherence to the Natyashastra. Our students range from young children taking their first steps in classical dance, to adults fulfilling a lifelong dream, to dedicated artists preparing for their Arangetram.

FAQ

Common questions

We welcome children from age 5 onwards. At this age, the focus is on basic posture, rhythm, and making dance joyful. Children who start young develop exceptional stamina, muscle memory, and a deep intuitive connection to the art form. However, it is never too late — we also have dedicated adult beginner batches.

Absolutely. We have a dedicated Adult Beginners batch with no upper age limit. Many of our most committed students began in their 30s and 40s. The pace is adapted for adult learning, and the experience is profoundly enriching — both physically and spiritually.

We maintain a strict maximum of 8 students per batch. This ensures that every student receives personal attention from Guru Deepa, and that corrections are specific, not generic. This small-batch philosophy is one of Kalpavruksha's core commitments.

We have morning and evening batches on weekdays and weekend morning batches. Specific timings and current fee structure are shared after the free trial class. We believe fees should be discussed in person so we can understand your goals and recommend the right batch. Please contact us or WhatsApp us to get the complete details.

The Arangetram is the classical solo debut performance — a milestone in a Bharatanatyam student's journey. It is a full-length, public recital of about 2.5–3 hours. Preparation typically takes 12–24 months of intensive focused training after a student has reached an advanced level. Kalpavruksha has guided over 50 Arangetrams with full support for music, costume, and production.

Yes. We offer live Online classes for students who cannot attend in person — whether you are based elsewhere in India or abroad. The classes are real-time (not pre-recorded), with the same rigour as in-person sessions. You will need a space of about 6×6 feet and a device with a good camera. Visit our Online Classes page for more details.
