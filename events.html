Our history

Highlights from 11 years

A timeline of milestones since Kalpavruksha was founded in 2014

2014
Founded

Kalpavruksha School of Fine Arts established

Smt. Deepa Sadasivam — having completed her Diploma from Kalakshetra Foundation and M.A. from the University of Madras — establishes Kalpavruksha School of Fine Arts in Kodambakkam, Chennai. The school’s name is drawn from the Kalpavruksha, the mythical wish-fulfilling tree of Indian tradition.

2016
Outreach

Kalakshetra Foundation outreach programme begins

Guru Deepa begins teaching at Chennai High School, Rangarajapuram, Kodambakkam, as an outreach faculty member from Kalakshetra Foundation — bringing classical Bharatanatyam to school students.

2017
Milestone

First Salangai Pooja at Kalpavruksha

The school’s first Salangai Pooja — the sacred ceremony in which a student’s ankle bells are tied for the first time — marking a major milestone for the school just three years after its founding.

2018
International

International performances — Malaysia & Singapore

Guru Deepa performs at Malaysia Kalai Sangaman, Batu Caves, and Mariyamman Temple, and in Singapore — bringing the Kalakshetra style to international audiences.

2019
Performances & milestones

Kanchipuram Athi Varadar Natyanjali & student recitals

Guru Deepa performs at the Kanchipuram Athi Varadar Natyanjali. Two Kalpavruksha students complete their first solo recitals. M.Phil in Bharatanatyam completed from Tamil Nadu Open University, Thanjavur.

2020
Innovation

Online classes launched

Kalpavruksha begins live online classes via Google Meet, expanding its reach to students in the UK, USA, and across India. The online programme quickly grows into a full-fledged offering with dedicated time slots for multiple time zones.

2021
Festival

Bridge Margazhi Festival 2021

A Kalpavruksha student performs a Bharatanatyam concert at the Bridge Margazhi Festival, streamed live to audiences worldwide.

2022
Festival & outreach

Bridge Margazhi 2022 & new outreach programmes

Continued participation at the Bridge Margazhi Festival. Guru Deepa joins Bridge Academy as Visiting Faculty and commences the DAV School Outreach Programme — two new platforms for reaching young students.

2023
Landmark year

First Arangetram & six Salangai Poojas

Kalpavruksha reaches a defining milestone: the school’s first Arangetram — a student’s classical solo debut — is performed. Six Salangai Poojas are guided in the same year. Guru Deepa also performs at Chidambaram, Vallakottam, Thirumayilai, Thiruporur, Parthasarathy Temple, Ashtalakshmi Temple, and Ekambaranathar Temple, Kanchipuram.

2024
Milestone

Three more Arangetrams

Three further students complete their Arangetrams in 2024, bringing the school’s total to four. Guru Deepa begins training under Smt. Sudharshini Iyer, continuing her own journey as a student of the art.

2025
26
Present

11 years of Kalpavruksha — 30+ Salangai Poojas

The school celebrates 11+ years of teaching, having guided 200+ students, completed 30+ Salangai Poojas, and mentored four students through their Arangetrams. Nineteen Salangai Poojas are held across three ceremonies in 2026 alone. The June 2026 batch opens for new enrolments.
