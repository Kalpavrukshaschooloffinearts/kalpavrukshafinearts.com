Our history

Highlights from 11 years

A selection of milestones from over a decade of teaching, performing, and celebrating Bharatanatyam

Students in performance — Margazhi festival season

2012

Visiting faculty begins

Smt. Deepa Sadasivam begins sharing her expertise in Kalakshetra-style Bharatanatyam as visiting faculty at various institutions in Chennai.

2014

Kalpavruksha founded

Smt. Deepa Sadasivam establishes Kalpavruksha School of Fine Arts in Kodambakkam, Chennai, with the vision of bringing authentic Kalakshetra-style Bharatanatyam to a new generation of students.

2017

First Arangetrams

Students of Kalpavruksha perform their Arangetrams — their classical solo debuts — marking a major milestone for the school. Guru Deepa serves as nattuvanar for the performances.

2019

Margazhi festival debut

For the first time, Kalpavruksha students perform at Chennai's Margazhi festival season — a landmark recognition of the school's artistic standards.

2020

Online classes launched

Kalpavruksha begins live online classes via Google Meet, expanding its reach to students in the UK, USA, and across India. The online programme quickly grows into a full-fledged offering.

2021

Bridge Margazhi Festival

Students perform at the Bridge Margazhi Festival 2021, with a Bharatanatyam concert by a student of Guru Deepa Sadasivam — streamed live to audiences worldwide.

2022

Bridge Margazhi 2022 & 2023

Continued participation in the Bridge Margazhi Festival, with student concerts earning wide appreciation. The school's online presence grows with performances reaching global audiences.

2025

11 years of Kalpavruksha

The school celebrates its 11th year of teaching, having guided 200+ students through their Bharatanatyam journeys, completed 20+ milestone ceremonies, and guided multiple Arangetrams.
