FAQ

Everything you need to know

We welcome children from age 5 onwards. At this age, the focus is on basic posture, rhythm, and joyful engagement with movement. Children who start young develop exceptional stamina and muscle memory. However, we also have adult beginner batches for those who come to dance later in life.

Absolutely. We have a dedicated Adult Beginners batch with no upper age limit. Many students began in their 30s and 40s. The experience is enriching both physically and personally. Adults bring maturity and dedication that often results in extraordinary progress.

A maximum of 8 students per batch. This is a non-negotiable policy at Kalpavruksha — we believe classical dance cannot be taught in a crowd. Every student must receive individual corrections and guidance.

We have morning batches (6–9 AM) on weekdays and mornings (6 AM – 12 PM) on weekends. Evening adult batches run 7–8:30 PM on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Exact timings depend on batch availability and are confirmed at enrolment. WhatsApp us for the current schedule.

Fees are discussed personally, as they depend on the course level, batch frequency, and whether you are attending in-person or online. We prefer to share fees after the free trial class, once we have a clear picture of your goals and the right programme for you. There are no hidden costs — all fees are transparent and paid per term.

For the trial class: comfortable clothing that allows movement (churidar / salwar is ideal). Barefoot is standard for Bharatanatyam. As you progress, you will need ghungroo (ankle bells) — we will advise on the right ones. Full classical costume is only needed for performances, not regular classes.

Yes. Students perform at the annual Salangai Pooja ceremony (typically in January) and at special school events. We also prepare students for graded examinations under recognised dance boards, if students wish to pursue certifications. Details are shared after enrolment.

Typically 6–10 years of regular training, depending on the student's frequency of practice, natural aptitude, and dedication. Arangetram is not a fixed timeline — it happens when the Guru assesses that the student is ready, both technically and artistically. We do not rush this milestone.

Yes — live Zoom classes for students globally. See our Online Classes page for full details on how they work, timezone accommodation, and requirements.

The Salangai Pooja is a sacred ceremony marking a Bharatanatyam student's initiation with the salangai (ankle bells). It is the first formal milestone in a student's dance journey, typically held after the first year of training. At Kalpavruksha, this is a celebrated annual event where all eligible students are honoured.
