FAQ

Everything you need to know

We welcome children from age 4 years onwards. At this age, the focus is on basic posture, rhythm, and joyful engagement with movement. Children who start young develop exceptional stamina and muscle memory. We also have dedicated adult beginner batches for those who come to dance later in life.

Absolutely. We have a dedicated Adult Beginners batch with no upper age limit. Many students began in their 30s and 40s. The experience is enriching both physically and personally. Adults bring maturity and dedication that often results in extraordinary progress.

Regular classes are held on weekday mornings for children and weekday evenings for adult batches. Classes meet 2 sessions per week in general, with each session lasting 1 hour. There are no weekend batches currently. Exact timings are confirmed at enrolment based on availability. Please WhatsApp us for the current schedule.

The Salangai Pooja is a sacred ceremony in which the student's ankle bells (salangai) are formally tied for the first time by the Guru. It is a deeply significant milestone — the student's first formal public performance — observed with traditional rituals, blessings, and celebration. At Kalpavruksha, the school has proudly guided over 20+ Salangai Poojas. It is an important prerequisite before the Arangetram journey begins. The timing of a student's Salangai Pooja is decided by the Guru based on readiness, not a fixed annual schedule.

The Arangetram is the classical solo debut performance — a full-length recital of approximately 2.5–3 hours, marking the student's transition from student to independent artist. Typically 6–10 years of regular training is required before a student is ready, though this depends on frequency and dedication. Arangetram preparation is an intensive, individualised process planned by the Guru based on programme needs. Kalpavruksha has guided 5+ Arangetrams.

Regular students participate in temple performances, festival events, and other school programmes as they progress. Salangai Pooja preparation, Arangetram preparation, and other intensive programme batches are planned individually based on the student's readiness — these are discussed separately with the Guru.

Fees are discussed personally after the trial class, as they depend on the course level and whether you are attending in-person or online. There are no hidden costs — all fees are transparent and paid per term.

For the trial class: comfortable clothing that allows free movement (churidar or salwar is ideal). Barefoot is standard for Bharatanatyam. As you progress, you will need ghungroo (ankle bells) — we will advise on the right ones. Full classical costume is only required for performances.
